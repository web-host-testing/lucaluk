Executive Summary

As the sole designer and researcher for revamping 22 school websites, I developed a unified template and design system that effectively aligned diverse user needs with business goals.

My deep empathy for users and strategic insight enabled the creation of targeted content strategies, even within tight constraints.

Through clear communication with client and vendors, we learned to avoid surprises and minimize rework, which led to a consistent and adaptable digital experience across all platforms.​​​

Brief

Align new branding across all 21 schools to present a unified and modernized online presence.

Problem

Outdated design and cluttered structure make school websites hard to navigate and information difficult to find.​​

Goals

  • Improve brand image
  • Increase student enrollment

Final Deliverables

Skip to Features

Designed an unified and customizable template for 21 schools and the network mothership site.

Project Background

Vast Information

Complex Network structure

Our client operates a network of 21 schools with varied sub-brands, each with its unique location and education level.

Varied Content and Design

With over 200 unique pages across sub-brands, each school maintains its distinct design and content.

Research

Impact with Minimal Resources

Leveraging Existing Insights

To maximize value within a limited budget, I focused on cost-effective research methods which provided in-depth insights without high expenses.

Prioritizing Strategic Needs

With the main goal of boosting student enrollment, I concentrated on research that directly influenced content strategy.

Market Reports

Provided broad insights into parent demographics and preferences, revealing key trends and behaviors.

Social Listening

Analyzed discussions on popular forums and social media to understand parental concerns and decision-making factors.

Root Cause Analysis

Integrated insights to uncover core parental motivations, focusing on ensuring a promising future for their children.

Competitor Analysis

Identify compelling UIs, features, and content that effectively attract parents to enroll their children.

Content Strategy

From Insights to Action

Aligning Content with Business Goals

By understanding parents’ motivations, I crafted a content strategy that aligns user needs with business goals, driving engagement and enrollment.

Actionable Game Plan

Each element of the plan, from specific data points to curated images, is designed to alleviate concerns, foster trust, and highlight our educational strengths.

Navigation

Information Architecture

Identifying Key Information Needs

We tailored content for different audiences, such as parents, students, and alumni, and tested with real users to ensure key information is accessible, even when streamlined across sub-brands.

Identifying Navigation Issues

Tree testing revealed unexpected issues where users relate certain very different pages together.

Learn parents' mental model

Through card sorting, we gained futher insights into how parents associate information with specific sections.

Referencing familiar structures

We referenced other schools’ site layouts to help users find key information quickly based on familiar designs.

Simplified Structure

We condensed the site from over 40 pages to 18 core pages, using tabs and direct links to simplify access, allowing users to easily locate the information or jump to related content.

Collaboration

Client Management

Aligning Expectations through Transparency

To ensure alignment and avoid rework, we recognize that clients may not always know what they need. By asking the right questions, we clarify their true objectives and keep communication open about our progress. ​

Content Collaboration

Collaborated closely with clients on content creation, using annotated wireframes to guide feedback and refine copywriting before development.

Development

IT Vendors

Challenges with Vendors Transitioning

After the first vendor failed to meet expectations, I guided the new vendor through a detailed project walkthrough, ensuring clear communication and reducing errors. This approach led to a well-documented design system, enhancing collaboration and aligning the new vendor with our standards.​

Design System

By leveraging my HTML and CSS expertise, I maintained close collaboration with the vendor on technical details, ensuring precise implementation of design specifications.

Solution

Features Highlights

Engaging and Easy-to-Build

The selected features create an engaging experience for parents, allowing them to quickly grasp key messages like their child’s bright future and the school’s strong network.

Quick Links

Display links that user likely look for on each page based on card sorting and tree test results.

Journey of Students

Helping parents envision their child's success at YCYW.

Simplified Enquiry Form

Reducing form-filling hassle of required fields from 20+ to 6 while gathering key information.

Optimize Campus Facilities VR

Assure parents that their children will study in best environment.

School Switching Dropdown

Introduce users to the whole network.

Interactive Map

Explain the structure of the sub-brands in the network.

Impact

Increase in Admission Enquiries

More parents showed interest in the schools.

Improved Branding

Unified branding enhanced public perception.

Enhanced Agency Reputation

As the biggest client by that time, the success of this project marked the beginning of local recognition.

What I Learned

Actionable Plan

I realized if we can't propose what to do for exact, the research can be a waste of time and resources.

Stakeholder management

Set small milestones with client and dev can greatly minimize the risk of redoing everything and ensure timely delivery.

Love in Complexness

This project required handling of huge amount of information, and I found myself enjoy this a lot. It is very satisfying to make complicated things easy for everyone.
